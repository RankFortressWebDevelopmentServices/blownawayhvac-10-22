Preventative Maintenance Tips For Your HVAC Unit

With summer in full swing, most people use their air conditioning to keep cool. But before you hit the switch and crank up the A/C, take a few minutes to perform preventative maintenance on your HVAC unit. By doing regular checks and cleaning, you can help ensure that your unit runs all season smoothly long – which could save you money on energy bills. So without further ado, Take a look at these tips to keep your HVAC unit running smoothly.

Changing your air filters regularly


Did you know that one of the best ways to prevent expensive HVAC repairs is to change your air filters regularly? It’s true! By changing your air filters regularly, you can prolong the life of your HVAC unit and keep it running smoothly. Most residential air filters need to be changed monthly, while commercial air filters may need to be changed more often.

Check the manufacturer’s recommendations to determine how often your air filters should be changed. When changing your air filters, follow the manufacturer’s directions carefully. If you don’t know how to change your air filters, contact a professional HVAC technician for help. Changing your air filters is an easy way to keep your HVAC unit running smoothly and help prevent expensive repairs. Follow the tips above to ensure you’re regularly changing your air filters.

Inspect the exterior of your unit for any damage or debris


Your HVAC unit is an essential part of your home, and it’s crucial to maintain it properly to ensure that it runs smoothly and efficiently all season long. One of the best ways to do this is by performing regular preventive maintenance. This includes inspecting the exterior of your unit for any damage or debris, cleaning the filters, checking the wiring, and more.

If you’re unsure how to perform preventive maintenance on your HVAC unit, plenty of resources are available online. Just be sure to follow the specific instructions for your particular unit. Regular preventative maintenance on your HVAC unit can help keep it running smoothly and efficiently all season long. It’s also a great way to avoid costly repairs down the road. So be sure to bookmark this page and return to it whenever you need guidance on maintaining your HVAC unit.

Clean the coils on the back of the unit at least once a year


If you want to make sure your HVAC unit is running smoothly, it’s essential to clean the coils on the back at least once a year. This is a simple task that anyone can do and will help keep your unit functioning properly. The coils on the back of the unit are where all the heat is released, so if they’re dirty, it can cause the team to work harder and may lead to premature failure.

To clean them, you’ll need a screwdriver to remove the cover, and then you can use a brush or vacuum to clean off the dirt and dust. It’s also important to check the filters regularly and replace them when they get dirty. A clogged filter can restrict airflow and cause the unit to work harder, leading to early failure. So make sure you change your filters regularly and keep those coils clean – it will help your HVAC unit run more efficiently and last longer.

Tighten all screws and bolts on the unit twice a year


Preventative maintenance is one of the most important things you can do for your HVAC unit. It’s simple and only takes a few minutes, but it can help to keep your unit running smoothly for years to come. One of the easiest things you can do is tighten all screws and bolts on the unit twice a year. This will help keep everything in place and prevent unnecessary wear and tear.

You should also make sure to clean the unit regularly. Dust and dirt can build up over time and cause the unit to work harder than necessary. A quick wipe-down every couple of months should be more than enough to keep things clean. Lastly, consult a professional if you have any questions or concerns about your HVAC unit. They can help identify potential problems and recommend solutions before they become more significant issues.

Make sure that the indoor unit is properly sealed to the window frame


A window air conditioning unit is a great way to cool a room without the expense of central air conditioning. However, if the unit is not sealed correctly, you may find that the cool air is escaping and your room is not being cooled as effectively as it could be. You can do a few things to ensure your window AC unit is properly sealed. First, ensure the unit is fitted correctly to the window frame.

The AC unit should cover the window frame entirely, with no gaps or spaces between the two. If there are any gaps, use weatherstripping or caulking to fill them in. Second, make sure that the window itself is appropriately sealed. If there are any cracks or holes in the window, use caulk or weatherstripping to seal them up. If you follow these tips, you can ensure that your window AC unit works as efficiently as possible and that you are getting the most out of your investment.

Schedule annual maintenance with a professional HVAC technician


Your HVAC unit is an essential part of your home, and it’s important to keep it in good condition with regular preventative maintenance. By scheduling annual maintenance with a professional HVAC technician, you can help ensure that your unit runs smoothly and efficiently all year. The technician will check the filters, coils, and other parts of the unit for any damage or wear and tear, and they’ll make any necessary repairs or adjustments. This can help extend the life of your HVAC unit and help avoid costly repairs down the road.

And, because preventative maintenance is typically less expensive than repairs, it can also save you money in the long run. So, schedule annual maintenance with a professional technician if you want to keep your HVAC unit running smoothly and efficiently. It’s an investment that will pay off in the long run. In addition to regular maintenance, you can also help keep your HVAC unit in good condition by having it serviced regularly. This will help ensure that any minor issues are caught and repaired before they become significant problems.

Summary


In summary, following the preventative maintenance tips we discussed in this blog post, you can keep your HVAC unit running smoothly and efficiently for many years to come. By taking good care of your unit, you will not only prolong its lifespan but also save money on energy bills and costly repairs down the road.
